This Site

This site is my personal online base. I will occasionally post about topics in statistics and neuroeconomics and mainly store and link to content regarding ongoing research projects, such as code-repositories and summaries of statistical analysis, and resources I deem relevant.

Me

I am currently a research assistant in the Rangel Neuroeconomics Laboratory at the California Institute of Technology. After a formal education in Business with focus on Finance, I made my way into the young science of Neuroeconomics, where I currently work on implementations and application of drift diffusion models to simple value based choices over multiple items. Our discipline broadly aspires to decode economic decision making combining research tools from neuroscience, neurobiology, cognitive psychology and behavioral economics. It turns out that the problems are harder to solve than combining the current knowledge of these disciplines into a new unified theory… not at last because the different disciplines involved historically missed a few weekly theory-compatitibility-checks, and ended up with vast amount of incongruous set of theories and predictions.
